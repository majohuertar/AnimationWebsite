Introduction

This website is a resource for people who want to learn more about animation, art references, and useful tools for artists. It brings together different websites, tutorials, and materials that help artists practice and improve their skills. The goal of this space is to make it easier to find helpful resources in one place, especially for beginners who may not know where to start.

This project was inspired by my own experience while learning the foundations of art. Many of the references and websites included here helped me understand important basics such as life drawing, anatomy, movement, and observation. These foundations are essential for animation because they help artists understand how bodies move, how gestures work, and how to give life to characters.

The website includes links to figure drawing tools, anatomy references, landscape and animal studies, and other practice resources that artists can use daily. It also highlights platforms that offer interactive models, tutorials, and exercises designed to build strong drawing habits. By exploring these resources, artists can practice consistently and develop their visual understanding.

More than just a list of links, this website acts as a small guide for learning and exploring art. It encourages experimentation, curiosity, and continuous learning. Whether someone is interested in animation, illustration, or general drawing practice, these resources can help build the skills needed to grow as an artist.
